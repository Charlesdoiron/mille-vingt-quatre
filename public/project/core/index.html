Core is a sensory and visual journey,

which implements a new technology of music spatialization with dynamic volumetric light.

Designed by 1024 architecture and premiered at the Electro exhibition at the Philharmonie de Paris 2019, CORE is located at the heart of the exhibition and references to computer processors, as well as the digital and scenographic visual components that acoompany most electronic musicians in concert. CORE transforms music into volumes of dynamic light, embodying in a spatial form, vibrating and oscillating to music.

Exhibited for the first time,

in the heart of the Electro Exhibition 2019 at the Philharmonie de Paris.

Where it illuminated an immersive room to the soundtrack of Laurent Garnier’s various mixes, inviting the guests to experience dynamic spatiality of light and sound in correspondenceâ€¨to each other.

 

The work takes the form

of a cube of 3 meters

Consisting of a matrix of 81 LED bars inclined 24° forward and implementing a new GLSL software created by 1024 together with partners Garage Cube, which is used in CORE to generatively produce spatial visualization of light.
